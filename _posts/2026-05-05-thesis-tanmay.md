On May 5th, Tanmay Chakraborty successfully defended his doctoral thesis on explainability and human–algorithm collaboration in Bayesian Optimization.
His research addresses a key challenge in industrial optimization: while Bayesian Optimization is highly data-efficient for complex black-box systems, its lack of transparency often limits practical adoption. The thesis introduces novel explanation methods designed specifically for Bayesian, including TNTRules, and MOLONE, which help experts understand optimization recommendations, parameter interactions, uncertainty, and trade-offs during decision-making.

The work further demonstrates through user studies that explanations significantly improve task performance, trust, and understanding without increasing cognitive load, highlighting the importance of explainability for effective human–AI collaboration in real-world optimization workflows.
